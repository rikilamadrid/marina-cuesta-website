# How to Update Your Site

Hi Marina — this is your whole website in your hands. You never need a developer to change what people see. Everything below can be done from your phone or laptop in about a minute each.

Your site lives at **marinacuesta.com**. You edit it from **marinacuesta.com/studio** — a private editor called the Studio.

---

## Signing in

1. Go to **marinacuesta.com/studio**.
2. Sign in with **GitHub** (the account you were invited with). *Not Google — even if it's the same email, Google is a different account and won't have access.*
3. You'll land in the Studio. On the left you'll see three things:
   - **Site Settings** — your name, photo, bio, contact, and social links.
   - **Work** — your projects.
   - **Press** — interviews, features, awards, and talks.

> **Tip:** Add the Studio to your phone's home screen so it opens like an app. It works great on mobile.

---

## The one rule that saves you: Publish

Nothing goes live until you press **Publish** (bottom-right, green button). You can edit freely, close the app, come back later — your changes are saved as a draft until you publish.

**When you publish, the change appears on your live site within a few seconds.** No waiting, no developer.

---

## Swap your headshot (one photo, everywhere)

Your photo is used in *one place* and flows automatically to your homepage, your About page, Google results, and link previews when someone shares your site. Change it once and it updates everywhere.

1. Open **Site Settings**.
2. Tap **Headshot** → remove the old image → upload the new one.
3. *(Optional but nice)* Drag the little circle on the photo to set the focal point, so it always crops around your face on small screens.
4. Press **Publish**.

Use a high-quality photo — ideally taller than it is wide (portrait), at least 1200px on the long side.

---

## Add a project

1. Click **Work** → **Create new** (the pencil / "+" at the top).
2. Fill in what you have. Only a few fields are required — the rest are optional:
   - **Project title** *(required)* — the name of the work.
   - **Web address** — press "Generate" to create it from the title.
   - **Client** *(required)* — e.g. GOYA, P&G.
   - **Category** *(required)* — pick one; this drives the filters on the Work page.
   - **Summary** *(required)* — one short, vivid line for the project card.
   - **Year, Market, Your role, Full write-up, Cover image, Gallery** — optional, add if you have them.
   - **Show on homepage** — turn on to feature it in "Selected Work" on the homepage.
3. Press **Publish**.

> **No cover image yet?** That's fine — the card shows a tasteful colored tile instead. You can add the image later.

**To reorder projects:** in the Work list, drag the rows into the order you want. That order is what visitors see.

---

## Add a press mention

1. Click **Press** → **Create new**.
2. Fill in:
   - **Headline** *(required)* — the title of the piece.
   - **Outlet** *(required)* — where it appeared, e.g. Adweek, Cannes Lions.
   - **Type** — Interview, Feature, Award, Talk, or Podcast.
   - **Date** — optional; shown as "June 2016."
   - **Link** *(required)* — the full web address, starting with `https://`.
3. Press **Publish**.

**To reorder press:** drag the rows in the Press list, same as Work.

There are a couple of placeholder entries in there now (e.g. "Add your interviews… here") — feel free to edit or delete them once you add real ones.

---

## Edit your bio or contact info

Everything about *you* lives in **Site Settings**:

- **Hero statement** — the line beside your name on the homepage.
- **Full bio** — the longer story on your About page.
- **Career arc** — the list of roles/milestones on About (drag to reorder).
- **Contact email** and **Social links** — shown in the Contact section.

Edit, then **Publish**.

---

## If something looks wrong

- **Did you press Publish?** Draft changes don't show on the live site until you do.
- **Give it a few seconds**, then refresh the page.
- Every change is versioned — in the Studio you can open a document's history and restore an earlier version, so you can't really break anything.

---

Anything beyond content — new sections, design changes, new page types — is a code change and needs a developer. But everything you'll actually want to keep current lives here, in your hands.
